The Way I Used To Be

$18.99 $20.99

The Way I Used to Be follows Eden, a high school senior whose life is shattered after a traumatic event. Struggling with feelings of isolation and shame, she navigates the complexities of her relationships and her identity while grappling with the aftermath of her experience. As Eden embarks on a journey of self-discovery, she learns to confront her pain and reclaim her voice, ultimately seeking healing and acceptance in a world that feels forever changed.

Author : Amber Smith

Highlights :

  • Plot Overview: The Way I Used to Be follows the story of Eden, a high school senior whose life is turned upside down after a traumatic event. Struggling with the aftermath of her experience, Eden grapples with feelings of isolation, shame, and confusion. As she navigates her relationships with friends and family, she finds herself slipping into a world of secrecy and denial. Throughout the novel, Eden embarks on a journey of self-discovery, learning to confront her pain and reclaim her voice. The narrative highlights her emotional struggles and resilience as she seeks to understand herself and her place in the world.
  • Themes : The book explores themes of trauma, healing, and the complexities of identity. It delves into the importance of self-acceptance, the impact of relationships on personal growth, and the journey toward finding one’s voice after experiencing trauma.
  • Genre: Young Adult, Contemporary Fiction
  • Pages: 368 pages
  • Publication Year: 2016

Why You’ll Love It

  • The story tackles difficult subjects with authenticity, providing insight into the struggles faced by survivors of trauma.
  • Eden’s journey of self-discovery resonates with readers, offering a compelling exploration of emotional resilience.
  • The narrative delves into complex emotions, making it a powerful read for those seeking a deeper understanding of healing.
  • The book's focus on identity and recovery encourages discussions about mental health, relationships, and personal growth.
